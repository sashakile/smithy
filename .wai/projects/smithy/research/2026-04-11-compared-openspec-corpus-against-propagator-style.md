Compared OpenSpec corpus against propagator-style computation. Main result: Smithy specs are strong on governance, determinism, and testable operational boundaries, but they are not propagator-native. Pipeline/cascade use centralized sequential orchestration with short-circuit outcomes; proposals/topology use linearizable audited state transitions; signals are telemetry for offline adaptation, not first-class monotone knowledge cells. Closest overlap is deterministic fold laws and explicit partial-result handling, but the system optimizes for controllable deployment rather than decentralized monotonic inference.
